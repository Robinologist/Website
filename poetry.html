Melting Down

– Nov 13, 2025 –

It’s not really about the wax, solidifying into the grain of my carpet as we speak. It’s not about the stain, even though the carpet was new and your novel embellishment of bright blue clashes like crazy with my rust-colored walls. I think if you took the time to scrape off each clumped tuft in apology, you would find that inside the pile of vivid clippings there is still an argument to be finished, something infuriatingly trivial and unwinnable as us two asses pull at the same rope in different directions. But it’s not really about that argument, frozen in opaque amber and saved for a rainy day when the heat of a different anger might defrost it, or about any of our arguments; the lighthouse, the piano rental, the holiday parking garage. Because in reality, I am annoyed about the wax. I’m annoyed that you thought to set up candles for our dinner and I didn’t, annoyed that I was late and let you down despite leaving work early, and annoyed that everything that could have possibly gone wrong did and our fresh start now smells like burnt rug.

Untitled

– Sep 26, 2025 –

There is a dark so profound that even silence is smothered.

A dark which melts down to smear across every crevice, every scrape and bump and divit

of my nails and of my face. It extinguishes the sound

of the drops dripping from my paddle to the pool. It extinguishes the sound

of my haggard prayers.

The prayers of a sinner seeking solace.

And from heaven there comes a call.

Down it falls, a word clattering along the rocks,

dangling on its string, bruised from the walls and too weak to fly by the time

it reaches me.

“Hello!” I crow back in its voice. “Hello! Hello!”

The sounds flee from my cracked lips;

I do not deserve them, they know this. They know. And so they rise,

shaking off the ties of my wretchedness

to return to paradise.

The miracle repeats, a new tattered blessing from above

gently crumples onto my ground.

And so I raise my joyous teeth to the sky and repeat it back.

“Echo! Echo! Echo!”

Daily Routine

– Oct 2, 2025 –

Torrid enforcements appear on my doorstep

With paper flaps wrapped ‘round a clinical prize –


Pale cartons brimming with shoals of ripped winnings

All tattered and squirming; I sharpen my knives.


Clockwork-like chopping; I hear the drip-dropping

Of clients arriving for liters of lies.


Feeding each thoroughly, sickly-sweet nurtures

Incur in my pertinent permanent brine.


Patiently waiting ‘till each one is sated

When finally facing oblation, they writhe.


Timers ring vibrant and chugging treads hide but

The giggling swell of my ice-cream truck’s chimes.

What You Will Not Find at the Baltimore Aquarium

– Sep 12, 2025 –

You will not find a building so big

you have to crane your head to frame it,

a comforting monolith full

of wonder and adorned

with winding windows

and waterfalls aching

to hold you in their courtyards

and carpets.

You will not find that spiral room that you remember,

a gentle slope leading down forever into

purple depths, lit dimly

like a movie theater showing

novel creatures from the pale floor,

and sparsely spaced with the odd

parent and child speaking in hushed tones.

There is no museum incongruous

whose white walls are the next room’s floors, where

one picture frame can contain a dark sea

of ancient fungi, endless in its own right,

and the next exhibit an indoor arboretum

of bioluminescent hills, all glazed

in colorful bulbs.

A food court and planetarium

on the hundredth floor, overlooking

the rest of the streaked rooftops

through wall-high windows while

star shapes spin and

savory plates clatter and

gargantuan silhouettes tear buildings apart outside

does not exist.

Nor is there a massive hedge maze

filled with piles of lightweight plane parts, broken

and discarded, like failed origami,

as cardboard dogfights light up

an overcast sky.

You cannot reach the busy metal-clad surroundings of

a bitter market, suspended within the din of

a heaving city, shifting, split

by sloping and

bending and

angled roads,

too steep to drive on without knowing

how to drive.

Yet you will still see that same

vivid smattering of sea-life

that colored in-between the lines

of your imagination

on your first visit, years ago

Convergent Evolution

– Sep 26, 2024 | Revised Oct 6, 2025 –

The tall intruder freezes at the sleeping floor’s creak;

He knows he's not welcome here


The little robber has done this before;

He believes he is entitled to his plunder


The tall one is frantic in his silence,

Not very silent at all


The other calmly cups nimble hands under running water,

So as to not leave dirty fingerprints


The tall intruder lurches through the back door,

Bounty seized in an erratic grasp


The little robber startles, dropping the lid and whirling

To face his assailant


Eyes meet:

Two thieves

Two masks

Scruffy hair and fur

Both matted by the rain

In one’s hand, a dangling locket on a silver chain

In one’s, a newly-liberated apple core.
